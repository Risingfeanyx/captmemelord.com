    Less is More

    Consider the following:

    With the rise of Windows 10's rolling release cycle, machines in a professional, or perhaps life-dependent environment are rendered useless, little more then a glorified Etch-a-Sketch

    *nix documentation is so excessive, it's built into the OS. Since any server worth it's silicon runs GNU/Linux, even Azure servers, anything you perfect locally will apply everywhere

    Windows cripples its users, the only reason it exists are three: existing support for AAA games, it comes preinstalled on most machines and Enterprise contracts.

    For many companies, it's easier to pay for a windows license knowing it'll come with support, and that's where many fail; poor management would rather give their people fish instead of teaching them how to fish
